Willy T. Ribbs

First African-American Driver in the Indianapolis 500

On May 19, 1991, William Theodore Ribbs broke the Indianapolis 500 color barrier by becoming the first African-American driver to qualify for the Greatest Spectacle in Racing. He qualified in 29th position with an average lap speed of 217.358 mph.

Unfortunately, Ribbs suffered an engine failure on the fifth lap and finished the 75th Indy 500 in 30th place.

Ribbs would go on to race in the Indy 500 again in 1993, qualifying in 30th position and finishing 21st.
